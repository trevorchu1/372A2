Driving through Germany

Ride at high speed down the Autobahn and spectate high stakes races at the Nürburgring! A perfect trip for all racing lovers. If you're more of a history buff, you can visit the BMW, Porsche, Mercedes-Benz, or Volkswagen museums. If you can make the trip in April, you may even be able to catch Techno Classica Essen, one of the largest classic automobile shows in the world! Travelling with someone special? The Romantic Road (Romantische Straße) is a scenic route that winds through southern Germany, passing picturesque towns, medieval castles, and beautiful countryside.

Exploring the Hidden Gems of Kyoto

Discover tranquil temples, secret gardens, and the best street food in Japan’s ancient capital. Our latest post dives deep into Kyoto’s lesser-known wonders. Fushimi Inari Taisha, famous for it's endless path of vermilion torii gates, is a must-see, especially during quiet hours of the day. West of the city, you can find Arashiyama, a district known for its scenic beauty and the iconic Bamboo Grove. Don't miss the chance to visit Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, which is stunning in any season.

Backpacking Through Patagonia

Patagonia is a magical destination for nature lovers. Torres del Paine National Park in Chile offers some of the most breathtaking hiking trails in the world. Be warned, the hikes are multi-day treks, so plan accordingly! In Argentina, the Los Glaciares National Park is home to the world famous Perito Moreno Glacier, which is one of the few glaciers in the world that is still growing. Puerto Natales is a charming base site for Torres del Paine, with resturants, gear shops, and breweries to prepare for the adventure. If you're planning for Perito Moreno, your launch point may be El Calafate, a small town with a lively center and plenty of steakhouses!
